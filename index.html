When my cousin came to me with a broken remote-control car and hoped I’d fix it, I intended to refuse because I did not know how a RC car worked, but his sincere request as a little child changed my mind to give it a try.

As I broke the RC car into its parts, I found a mini-world inside the car. Every piece seemed independent of the others, but combined, they performed unbelievably complex tasks. The RC car was like the Earth, electronic components in the car were like humans and buildings, and the wires resembled oceans. Just like humans want to explore everything around them, I wanted to understand how this RC car worked and why it worked. Thereafter, I found my passion.

After I fixed the car, I felt more purposeful and determined about continuing my expedition in science. Subsequently, I made a Bluetooth-Controlled car from scratch for my cousin, upgraded it to a hand-controlled car as a 2.0 version, started to make speakers with my friends, became the Math Club leader, co-created a WeChat Official Account called “AKA Science”, and began to work on this website.

I’ll admit that I’ve been searching for my true passion for too long and in that process, and I used to flinch in front of challenges and difficulties because I simply did not know where to start. However, now, I realize that problems can be solved if I break them down to pieces and start small. I’m not afraid anymore. I’m ready to explore.

Thanks for dropping in! Kevin Huang, Class of 2021.
